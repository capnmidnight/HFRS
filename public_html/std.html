Every institution has it: their database clean-up wish list. When things slow down for a few weeks, all those code tables will be cleansed, superfluous Attributes deleted, phone type sharing reconfigured, the list goes on. But things never slow down, and the list keeps growing. Better data is not a luxury. Working around clunky, redundant or inaccurate information affects your ability to report and can even endanger your relationship with your constituents. Our dedicated staff will assess your database and develop a plan to optimize your data - best of all, we'll put the plan into action for you so you can keep doing what you do best. Does this look like your wish list?

  • Addressees/Salutations - Your envelopes say "Mr. and Mrs. Johnson" but your letter reads "Dear Mr. Bob," ... what?
  • International Addresses - Are you mailing to 21 Comrade Street, Moscow, Russia 103073? Wonder why you keep getting billed for return postage?
  • Table Cleanup - Are your solicit codes redundant and/or unsubstantiated? Are your field tables riddled with redundancies?
  • Profile Completion - Does every individual (constituent and non-constituent) have a Preferred Name, Title and Gender? If you're using dates on Constituent Codes, does everyone have them?
  • Fund Architecture - Do your Fund IDs match the Business Office's general ledger numbers? Can you accurately report on matching gifts that cross fiscal years?
